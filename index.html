СПРАВОЧНИК | РЕСУРСЫ
БАЗА ЗНАНИЙ И ШАБЛОНЫ
I'm diagnosed with ADHD and I've been lifting consistently for three years. A lot of people ask me how do you stay so consistent when you have ADHD?
The answer is that I do habits every single day. A lot of people say "hit the gym three to five times a week and you're good to go" and "use a habit tracker to remember it". But the issue with that is, first of all, with ADHD remembering the fact that you have a habit tracker is a whole another thing and I've never was able (I have never been able / I was never able) to stick to that.
So instead, what I do is that, whenever i wanna build a habit, i just do it every single day, so that I don't have to remember which days I actually did the habit. And also it removes the decision fatigue as well.
When you're only doing something three to five times a week, then there are gonna be days that you don't do it. And every single morning, when you wake up, you have to decide whether today is a do day or not to do day.
So when you remove that and when you do the thing every single day then you don't have to decide which days that you do the thing. You might be thinking: "does it mean I'm not gonna take a rest?". No, you can take a rest. But the important thing is that you still go to the gym. Even if you don't lift weights.
For example: you can do a stretch, you can do a five-minute walk. Whatever that is, the point with ADHD and building habits is that you do the thing every single day and you adjust the amount of how much or how intense you do the thing. Based on the day, you know: how you're feeling, how hard you can push.
